\begin{frame}{Video Generation: From media synthesis to world simulators}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item[\textcolor{red}{$\blacktriangleright$}] A useful split today: systems that prioritize high-fidelity pixels and instruction following (e.g.\ large text-to-video diffusion) versus systems that prioritize compact latent dynamics for control (e.g.\ JEPA-style prediction).
    \item[\textcolor{red}{$\blacktriangleright$}] Video remains expensive: long tensors and coherent motion are harder than sharp stills; evaluation must separate perceptual quality from physical plausibility.
    \item[\textcolor{red}{$\blacktriangleright$}] The frontier is shifting from realistic-looking clips to models that approximate causal structure and action consequences---what curricula often bundle under \emph{world models}.    
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
