\begin{frame}{Summary}
  \begin{itemize}\footnotesize
    \item \textbf{JEPA: A new paradigm for Self-Supervised Learning}\\
    Joint Embedding Predictive Architectures (JEPA) learn \emph{internal world models} by predicting \textbf{abstract representations} of masked or future regions, instead of raw data, enabling robust and efficient learning.
    \item \textbf{Contrast to Classic SSL:} JEPA unifies ideas from contrastive (invariant) and generative SSL, but avoids pixel-level prediction.
    \item \textbf{Strong Transfer:} Predicting stable, high-level embedding targets yields representations that excel at downstream tasks such as VQA, reasoning, and planning.
    \item \textbf{Flexible Recipe:}\\
    The JEPA framework extends to images (I-JEPA), video (V-JEPA, V-JEPA 2), point clouds (Point-JEPA), vision transformers and CNNs (CNN-JEPA), and multimodal data (VL-JEPA).
    \item \textbf{Active Areas:} Ongoing work explores efficient teachers (SALT), hierarchical and recurrent world models, multimodal fusion, and theoretical understanding of latent prediction.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
